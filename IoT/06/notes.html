Why are Bluetooth Classic and BLE considered different protocols? What are their typical use cases?
  • They differ in spectrum usage, packet formats, and data exchange schemes — not just power levels.
  • Bluetooth Classic: Higher throughput, higher energy consumption → suited for continuous audio streaming, file transfer.
  • BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy): Lower energy, lower data rate → suited for IoT sensors, wearables, beacons, periodic small data transmissions.

How does BLE organize the 2.4 GHz band, and why is this structure useful?
  • BLE uses ~80 MHz of the 2.4 GHz band, divided into 40 channels × 2 MHz:
  • 37 data channels – used for bidirectional data transfer after connection.
  • 3 advertising channels (channels 37, 38, 39) – used for device discovery, broadcasting, and connection setup.
  • This separation allows efficient discovery/connection setup independent of data traffic, with good coexistence and Adaptive Frequency Hopping (AFH) on data channels to avoid interference (e.g., Wi-Fi).
