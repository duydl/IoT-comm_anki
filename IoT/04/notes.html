You need Wi-Fi for a small apartment with many walls. Which band gives better coverage, and which gives better speed in a less crowded area? Explain briefly.
  • 2.4 GHz: Better coverage and wall penetration, but more interference (Bluetooth, microwaves, other Wi-Fi) and generally slower.
  • 5 GHz: Less interference and higher throughput potential, but shorter range and weaker penetration.

Name one key advantage and one disadvantage of IEEE 802.11a over 802.11b.
  • Advantage: Higher theoretical data rate (54 Mbps) and uses the less crowded 5 GHz band.
  • Disadvantage: Shorter range due to the higher operating frequency (5 GHz attenuates faster than 2.4 GHz).

What are two major PHY/MAC improvements introduced in 802.11n / 802.11ac that increase throughput?
  • MIMO – multiple antennas / spatial streams for parallel data transmissions.
  • Wider channels – 40 MHz (11n), 80 / 160 MHz (11ac) instead of 20 MHz.
  • Frame aggregation (A-MPDU / A-MSDU) – MAC-level bundling reduces per-frame overhead.
  • Higher-order QAM – e.g., 256-QAM in 802.11ac encodes more bits per symbol.

Why is OFDM widely used in Wi-Fi? Mention one benefit related to multipath.
  • OFDM splits data across many narrow subcarriers; each subcarrier experiences a nearly flat channel → easier equalization.
  • A guard interval (cyclic prefix) is inserted between symbols, which absorbs multipath delay spread and eliminates inter-symbol interference (ISI).
  • Modulation/demodulation is efficiently done via IFFT / FFT.

What is the purpose of the spectral mask in IEEE 802.11?
  • The spectral mask defines the maximum allowed transmitted power at various frequency offsets from the channel center frequency.
  • It ensures the signal does not spill excessively into adjacent channels, limiting co-channel and adjacent-channel interference.
  • Compliance is mandatory for regulatory approval and coexistence in shared spectrum.
