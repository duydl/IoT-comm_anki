What are the major limitations of LoRaWAN that motivate the development of DSME-LoRa / 6LoRa?
  • Unreliable uplink communication – no guaranteed delivery in standard LoRaWAN.
  • Restricted downlink communication – downlink windows are narrow and infrequent.
  • No peer-to-peer (P2P) communication – all traffic must go through a gateway/server.
  • Requires permanent infrastructure backhaul – cannot work without a gateway connected to a network server.
  • These motivate a more flexible, reliable MAC (DSME) and full IPv6 integration (6LoRa) to enable multi-hop and direct device-to-device communication.

What is the role of Channel Activity Detection (CAD) in DSME-LoRa, and why is it beneficial?
  • LoRa was not originally designed for carrier sensing, but CAD acts as a practical clear-channel assessment method: the radio checks for LoRa preamble activity on the channel before transmitting.
  • Benefits:
  • Packet Reception Rate (PRR) can improve by up to ~17%.
  • Retransmissions can drop by up to ~95%.
  • A single CAD operation often costs less energy than a retransmission (net energy saving).
